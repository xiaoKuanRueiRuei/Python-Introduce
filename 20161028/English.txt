Modern English has little inflection compared with many other languages, 
and relies more on auxiliary verbs and word order for the expression of complex tenses, 
aspect and mood, as well as passive constructions, interrogatives and some negation. 
Despite noticeable variation among the accents and dialects of English used in different countries and regions ¡V in terms of phonetics and phonology, 
and sometimes also vocabulary, grammar and spelling ¡V English-speakers from around the world are able to communicate with one another with relative ease.
